Why Memristors?

With the recent rise of Large Language Models and other large-scale machine learning projects, the extreme amount of power used by these systems has resulted in both cost and enviormental concerns. With some estimating ChatGPT-3 queries resulting in 1GWh of power consumption per day, developing more efficient methods of performing neural network inference calculations as grown into a 100 million dollar problem. I'm interested in memristor-based computing because I believe it may be key to the long term stability of artifical intelligence projects. By arranging memristors into a crossbar array, matrix multiplications can be performed using basic circuit principles instead of flipping transistors, resulting in savings in speed and power consumption.

Why Computer Engineering?

I've always wanted to understand how the things around me worked, but without formal education, electrical and computer systems often just look like magic. Throughout my time in college, I've been learning more and more about the design of computer hardware, and I hope to one day harness that knowledge to build something that will, in some small way, change the world.
